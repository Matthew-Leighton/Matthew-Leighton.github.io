Research Projects

Stochastic modelling of cellular Salmonella infection.

Worked with professor Andrew Rutenberg on mathematical and computational modelling of host-pathogen dynamics. More specifically, we developed an agent-based stochastic model for invasion of host cells by Salmonella bacteria. Using this model we were able to gain a quantitative understanding of the impact of the ruffling mechanism on the statistics and dynamics of invasion. A paper detailing these results is currently begin finalized.

Modelling Intermolecular Cross-Linking in Collagen Fibrils.

Currently working on a coarse grained liquid-crystal elastomer model of cross-linked collagen fibrils as part of an honours thesis with professor Andrew Rutenberg. The goal is to better understand how intermolecular cross-linking determines the elastic properties of a fibril.
