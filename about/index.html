I grew up in Vancouver, but now live in Halifax. I'm in my fourth (and final) year of study at Dalhousie University, working on a bachelor's degree with honours in physics and mathematics. I'm really interested in the application of quantitative tools from the fields of physics and mathematics to complex problems in biology. My previous research has focused on using quantitative stochastic modelling to investigate cooperativity in the invasion process of Salmonella bacteria. Currently, as part of my honours research project, I am modelling the effects of strain on cross-linked collagen fibrils in an effort to understand the interplay between intermolecular cross-linking and the elastic properties of fibrils.
